Fabrics




What are the types of Fabrics?

1.Silk
2.Cotton
3.Wool
4.Polyester
5.Denim
6.Leather




Silk




Silk is a natural fiber produced by silkworms. It is made from the cocoons spun by the larvae of the silk moth, known scientifically as Bombyx mori. The silk production process begins with the cultivation of silkworms and ends with the extraction of silk threads from the cocoons.

Cotton




Cotton is a seed-hair fiber made mostly of cellulose. The fibers are composed of about 87 to 90 percent cellulose (a carbohydrate plant substance), 5 to 8 percent water, and 4 to 6 percent natural impurities.

Wool




Wool fabric is a cloth made from the fleece of sheep and other animals. It's a natural, durable, and versatile material that's been used for thousands of years.

Polyester




Polyester is a synthetic fabric made from petroleum-based chemicals. It's a type of plastic that's used in many applications, including clothing, home furnishings, and industrial products.

Denim




Denim is a sturdy, durable, and versatile fabric made from cotton that is woven in a twill pattern. It is often dyed with indigo, but can also be dyed with other colors. Denim is used to make many types of clothing, including jeans, jackets, dresses, and overalls.

Leather




Leather fabric is a natural material made from animal hides that have been tanned and treated to preserve them. It's known for being durable, water resistant, and insulating.
